What is CRAB AGENT?

CRAB AGENT is an AI that watches the Solana meme coin market for you, all day, every day. Instead of you staring at charts trying to spot the next mover, the AI does that watching and hands you a simple signal: BUY, HOLD, or AVOID.

Think of it like having someone scan hundreds of coins for you, non-stop, and only tap you on the shoulder when something looks genuinely worth a look.

How does CRAB AGENT find coins?

It watches the market itself, not a list of keywords. It picks up real activity happening right now on Solana - coins getting boosted, new and updated projects, community activity, and whatever categories are actually trending at the moment.

You can still search for a specific coin by name if you want to check on something yourself - but the coins that show up automatically on your dashboard got there because something real is happening with them, not because they matched a search term.

Worth knowing: this means CRAB AGENT won't necessarily show every coin that's trending elsewhere. A coin can be actively traded and still not appear here yet if it hasn't been boosted, hasn't updated its profile, or isn't part of a currently-trending category - it isn't a mirror of every screener out there, it's watching its own specific set of real market signals.

What is CRAB SCORE?

CRAB SCORE is a single number from 0 to 100 that sums up how a coin looks right now - things like how much real trading is happening, how healthy its liquidity is, how big (or small) its valuation is, and whether the price action looks like a genuine build-up rather than a coin that already ran.

You don't need to know how the number is built. Just know: higher generally means healthier, and the AI already weighs the messy parts for you.

Signal Legend - What Each Tier Means

STRONG BUY Healthy, still-building conditions with high confidence behind them.
BUY Good conditions overall, but with somewhat less certainty than a STRONG BUY.
HOLD Not bad, but not confirmed enough yet - worth watching, not worth acting on.
AVOID Risk currently outweighs the potential - liquidity, timing, or selling pressure looks concerning.

Two coins can share the same tier and still look quite different underneath. A BUY with 80% Signal Strength and a BUY with 55% Signal Strength are both genuinely BUY, but the first one has more evidence behind it. Always glance at Signal Strength and the detail breakdown, not just the badge color.

Why isn't every high score a BUY?

A high score means the coin looks healthy overall - but timing matters just as much as health. A coin can look great on paper and still be a bad time to buy, for example if it already pumped hard in the last few hours. The AI checks for that separately, and if the timing looks off, it will hold back a BUY even on a coin with a decent score.

This is on purpose. The goal isn't to find "good coins" after the fact - it's to catch them while they're still building, not after they've already moved.

What does Signal Strength mean?

Signal Strength tells you how much the AI actually trusts the signal it just gave you. A BUY with high Signal Strength means the data behind it is solid and consistent. A BUY with lower Signal Strength means the same recommendation, but with less certainty behind it - worth being a bit more careful with.

What is Confidence?

Confidence is closely related to Signal Strength - it reflects how complete and how consistent the information the AI had to work with actually was. Thin or contradictory data lowers Confidence, even if the numbers themselves look exciting. Think of it as the AI telling you "here's how sure I am," not just "here's what I found."

What is Early / Mid / Late?

This is the AI's read on timing specifically - separate from the score itself. Early means the move looks like it's just getting started. Mid means it's already underway. Late means it's likely already played out, even if the score still looks okay. This is one of the most important things to check before acting on any signal.

How are BUY signals created?

The AI is specifically built to avoid handing out a BUY signal after a coin has already pumped. It looks for healthy, still-building conditions - steady volume growth, real buyers stepping in, a trend that isn't already stretched - rather than rewarding a coin just because it moved a lot already. A BUY should ideally catch a coin while it's building, not after it has exploded.

Why can a score change?

The market moves, so the score moves with it. A coin can go from HOLD to BUY because real buying picked up, or from BUY to AVOID because the move already extended further than is healthy. This is exactly why CRAB AGENT keeps scanning continuously instead of giving you a one-time answer.

How often does the AI refresh?

The AI rescans the market roughly every 60 seconds. You don't need to manually refresh anything - new opportunities appear, and existing ones update, on their own.

Why do some coins disappear?

A coin drops off the dashboard when it no longer meets basic health checks - for example its liquidity dried up, trading activity died down, or it stopped showing up in any of the real market feeds the AI watches. Disappearing isn't necessarily a bad sign about that specific coin, it usually just means it's no longer a good fit for what CRAB AGENT is built to surface right now.

What does BUY mean?

The AI sees healthy, still-developing conditions and believes now looks like a reasonable time to consider an entry. It is not a guarantee - it's a read on the current data.

What does HOLD mean?

The coin doesn't look bad, but the AI isn't confident enough - either about the coin's health or the timing - to call it a BUY yet. HOLD generally means "worth watching, not worth acting on yet."

What does AVOID mean?

Something about the coin's current condition looks risky - this could be an already-overextended price move, signs of heavy selling, thin liquidity, or very little real trading activity. AVOID is the AI telling you the risk currently outweighs the potential.

Where does the data come from?

Everything comes from live, public Solana market data - the same on-chain activity anyone can see, just watched continuously and summarized for you automatically instead of you having to track it yourself.

Can the AI be wrong?

Yes. CRAB AGENT reads real market data and applies a consistent process to it, but it cannot predict the future and it cannot see things that haven't happened yet. Meme coins are volatile by nature - treat every signal as a well-informed read on the current situation, not a certainty.
